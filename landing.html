Step 1 — Foundation

What is Enterprise Architecture?

Every organisation — a hospital, a bank, a government agency — runs on thousands of interconnected decisions about technology, process, and people. When those decisions are made inconsistently, without a shared model of where the organisation is going, the result is technical debt, failed programmes, and systems that work against each other instead of together.

Enterprise Architecture is the discipline that makes those decisions well. Not by drawing diagrams first and hoping someone reads them. Not by producing 200-page documents that arrive after the decision is already made. By asking a simpler question first:

"What decision are we actually making, who needs to make it, and what is the minimum information we need to make it well?"

An enterprise architect is not a diagrammer or a documenter. They are a decision navigator — someone who identifies the choices that will shape a system, brings the right people into the room, and ensures the outcome is recorded so the same debate doesn't happen again next quarter.

Architecture practice spans every sector: financial services, government, health, defence, utilities, and technology. It operates at every level: from a single application decision to a billion-dollar transformation programme. What stays constant is the core discipline — making decisions well, at the right time, with the right evidence.

Step 2 — The Problem

Why was VAF built?

Traditional enterprise architecture methods were designed for a slower world. Three structural problems appear repeatedly across organisations that follow conventional EA practice.

Problem 01

Decisions happen before architecture does

By the time a traditional EA team produces a document, the delivery team has already moved. The architecture artefact describes a past state — it doesn't shape the future. The business made the call in a meeting two weeks ago.

Problem 02

Documents describe — they don't decide

Most architecture output shows what currently exists: landscape diagrams, capability maps, as-is assessments. These describe the landscape but don't frame a specific choice. The person who needs to make a decision gets a map, not a recommendation.

Problem 03

Rationale disappears

Why a decision was made vanishes into email threads and meeting notes. Six months later, a new team member asks why the system works this way, and no one can answer. The same debate restarts in the next programme — from scratch.

VAF was built to solve all three — not by adding more process, but by inverting the model: decisions first, then the minimum artefact that makes that decision well.

Step 3 — The Framework

VAF Core: Decision-First Architecture

The Velocity Architecture Framework™ organises all architecture work around decisions, not documents. Here is the core concept and the six viewpoints that make up the full framework.

Traditional EA

Documents → Decisions

Produce artefacts first. Present to stakeholders. Hope a decision follows. Artefacts are often comprehensive, generic, and late.

VAF — Decision-First

Decisions → Minimum Artefacts

Identify the decision to be made. Produce only the artefact that frames that specific decision. Everything else is waste until needed.

The Six VAF Viewpoints

VAF organises architecture practice into six viewpoints. Each viewpoint produces a small set of targeted artefacts — 18 artefacts in total — each anchored to a decision type, not a document convention.

Viewpoint 01

Governance

Who decides what, how decisions are made, and how they are recorded and traceable over time. The accountability layer of the framework.

Artefacts: Decision Register, Architecture Authority Model, Governance Charter

Viewpoint 02

Strategy

Where the organisation is heading and the architectural choices that make that direction achievable. Business strategy translated into architecture intent.

Artefacts: Direction Statement, Capability Map, Strategic Architecture Brief

Viewpoint 03

Design

The solution shape: components, integrations, and the technical decisions that define how the system will be built and operated.

Artefacts: Solution Overview, Architecture Decision Record, Integration Design

Viewpoint 04

Delivery Velocity

How the architecture enables fast, low-risk delivery. Identifies the sequencing, dependencies, and constraints that affect delivery pace.

Artefacts: Delivery Architecture, Risk & Constraint Register, Sequencing Model

Viewpoint 05

Operating Rhythm

How the architecture team stays synchronised with delivery teams. Defines the cadence, touchpoints, and communication patterns for effective architecture governance.

Artefacts: Engagement Model, Review Cadence, Architecture Radar

Viewpoint 06

Practitioner Capability

How architects continuously improve their own practice. Covers skills development, tooling, and the frameworks architects use to assess and grow their own capability.

Artefacts: Capability Profile, Practice Maturity Assessment, VSF Score

Step 4 — Standards Context

VAF and TOGAF 10

TOGAF is the world's most widely adopted enterprise architecture framework, used by thousands of organisations globally. VAF is not a replacement — it is a compatible, open-source alternative built for practitioners who need architecture at delivery speed.

Dimension TOGAF 10 Velocity Architecture Framework™
Core philosophy Process-first — follow the ADM phases to produce architecture Decision-first — identify the decision, then produce the minimum artefact
Artefact volume Comprehensive — 100+ document types across the content framework Minimal — 18 targeted artefacts across 6 viewpoints
Speed Weeks to months per architecture cycle Hours to days — board-ready packs from a brief
Cost of entry Paid certification required (Foundation + Practitioner) Open source — free to learn, use, and adapt
Standards alignment The Open Group standard ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2011 aligned · TOGAF ADM compatible
AI readiness Not designed for AI-assisted artefact generation Built for agentic AI — prompts and agents produce VAF outputs natively
Governance model Proprietary — The Open Group Community-governed — open source, publicly versioned
Important: VAF and TOGAF are not mutually exclusive. Many practitioners use both — TOGAF for institutional compliance and stakeholder communication, VAF for day-to-day decision-speed practice. If your organisation is TOGAF-certified, VAF adds velocity without replacing your existing investment. If you are learning architecture for the first time, VAF is the faster path to practical capability.

VSF Dimensions

Five dimensions. Every architecture role measured the same way.

Each dimension is weighted to reflect its relative importance in enterprise architecture career progression. Scores run 1.0–10.0, derived from evidence in your CV measured against the specific job description.

01

Scale of Impact

25%

How far does your work reach? This dimension measures the organisational and financial scope of your architectural contributions — users affected, systems integrated, geographies spanned, and dollars at risk.

8–10Multi-national programmes, 1M+ users, $100M+ financial consequence
6–8Enterprise-wide transformation, $10M+, hundreds of thousands of users
4–6Divisional or business-unit scope, $1–10M programmes
2–4Team or project-level, limited organisational footprint
02

Complexity Governed

25%

How difficult was the environment you operated in? Measured by simultaneous complexity vectors: M&A integration, regulatory compliance (APRA, GDPR, ISM, Essential Eight), live production constraints, multi-vendor ecosystems, and geographic distribution.

8–10Multiple simultaneous vectors — M&A + regulatory + multi-cloud + legacy migration
6–8Two or three significant complexity factors active concurrently
4–6Standard enterprise complexity, single-domain or single-platform
2–4Greenfield or well-defined single-vendor environment
03

Authority Held

20%

What real decision-making power did you exercise? This dimension distinguishes between advising and deciding — it looks for formal governance roles: Architecture Review Board chair, Design Authority lead, board-level presentations, and investment sign-off authority.

8–10Formal architecture authority with C-suite/board engagement and investment sign-off
6–8ARB chair or Design Authority lead with regular executive presentations
4–6Senior technical lead with governance influence, advisory capacity
2–4Individual contributor with recommendations only, no formal authority
04

Outcome Integrity

20%

Did the work actually land? Architecture is only valuable when it produces outcomes. This dimension looks for on-time delivery, zero-disruption migrations, measurable business results, and clean programme handovers — not just plans and artefacts.

8–10Multiple major programmes delivered on time and on budget with documented outcomes
6–8Consistent delivery evidence with specific, citable results
4–6Mixed or partially evidenced outcomes, some delivery without specifics
2–4Limited outcome evidence or theoretical/incomplete delivery record
05

Capability Transferred

10%

What did you leave behind? The most senior architects build organisational capability — frameworks, patterns, documented standards, team uplift, and training. This dimension measures the durable, transferable value of your contribution beyond the immediate engagement.

8–10Enterprise-wide frameworks or standards adopted broadly; significant team capability uplift
6–8Patterns or frameworks created and adopted; coaching or mentoring with evidence
4–6Some documentation, knowledge sharing, or structured training delivered
2–4Individual contribution only, minimal durable knowledge transfer

Benchmark Bands

Where do you stand?

The VSF weighted score (sum of dimension × weight) places you in one of five benchmark bands calibrated to the Australian enterprise architecture market. These bands align with real hiring expectations at each level.

3.0 – 4.5 Graduate / Junior EA Early-career practitioner. Works within defined scope under supervision. Limited independent governance or delivery track record.
4.5 – 6.0 Mid-level Solution Architect Independent contributor delivering within a domain. Emerging governance influence. Evidence of delivered outcomes at project scale.
6.0 – 7.5 Senior Solution Architect / Lead SA Operates at programme level. Cross-domain influence. Regular executive engagement. Consistent delivery record with measurable business outcomes.
7.5 – 8.5 Principal / Lead Enterprise Architect Enterprise-wide influence. Formal architecture governance authority. Board or C-suite level engagement. Sustained track record at scale and complexity.
8.5 – 10.0 Chief Architect / Distinguished Engineer Organisational architecture ownership. Sets direction across multiple domains. Investment authority. Leaves enduring frameworks and capability uplift.

Standards & Governance

ISO Aligned. TOGAF 10 Compatible. Open Source.

The Velocity Architecture Framework™ and the VSF career measurement system are built on open, interoperable principles aligned with internationally recognised architecture standards.

ISO / IEC / IEEE 42010

Architecture Description Standard

ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2011 is the international standard for Systems and Software Engineering — Architecture Description. VAF aligns with its core architectural concepts:

  • Architecture viewpoints and views
  • Stakeholder concerns and system context
  • Architecture decisions and rationale (ADRs)
  • Correspondence and consistency rules
TOGAF 10

Enterprise Architecture Compatibility

The Velocity Architecture Framework is structurally compatible with TOGAF 10 while adopting a lighter, decision-first operating model suited to modern transformation velocity:

  • Architecture Development Method (ADM) alignment
  • Architecture Content Framework compatibility
  • Enterprise Continuum and Repository concepts
  • Architecture Governance principles
Open Governance

Community-Governed Framework

VAF is open-source and community-governed — designed for practitioners, teams, and institutions that need TOGAF-compatible practice without proprietary certification dependency:

  • Free to use, adapt, and extend
  • No vendor lock-in or proprietary tooling
  • Suitable for academic and institutional adoption
  • Transparent versioning and public artefacts

Open-Ground TOGAF 10 Positioning. VAF is positioned as a next-generation, open-ground alternative for practitioners, universities, and enterprise teams who require a structured EA methodology without the cost and rigidity of proprietary TOGAF certification pathways. It preserves TOGAF 10's conceptual architecture while delivering a practitioner-first, decision-speed operating model — making it suitable for adoption as an open institutional alternative or complement to formal TOGAF 10 training. The Velocity Architecture Academy provides structured learning, practitioner certification, and institutional delivery pathways built on these foundations.
